6 linking rules

RULE 1
Every article to homepage
Every supporting article must link back to the homepage at least once using descriptive anchor text — never "click here".
RULE 2
Every article to its pillar hub
Every article links to its cluster hub using relevant anchor text. Shows Google the topic tree and concentrates authority.
RULE 3
Pillar to all cluster articles
Each pillar page must link down to every article in its cluster. Distributes equity and signals the full topic hierarchy.
RULE 4
Homepage outgoing links
Homepage links to exactly 7 cluster hub pages — one per main cluster — with no more than 12 total outgoing links.
RULE 5
Sibling sideways links
Every article links sideways to 2-3 related articles within the same cluster using natural anchor text. Builds topic authority.
RULE 6
Unique anchor text always
Never use the same anchor text twice on the same page. Vary anchor text across all links to maintain relevance signals.

Reference Guide
💡 What is Semantic SEO?
Semantic SEO is optimizing content so search engines deeply understand the meaning, context, and relationships between topics — not just matching keywords. It helps Google understand your content like a human expert would.
🏷️
What are Entities?
Entities are specific people, places, things, brands, products, concepts, or organizations that have a clear, unambiguous meaning. Google uses entities to understand the real-world subjects your content is about.
⚗️
The Semantic SEO Formula
Main Keyword + Entities + Related Concepts + User Questions + Context = ✨ Semantic Content
✍️
Semantic Sentence Formula
Use:
Subject + Action + Related Entity + Outcome
